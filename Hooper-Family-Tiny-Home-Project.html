📊 The Opportunity

The housing gap is real. The demand is documented. The land is ours.

The question was simple: could our family's land support something that makes money and lasts? The research came back strong. Brunswick County is the fastest-growing county in North Carolina, there's a documented housing shortage, and there's almost nothing available for working professionals and families who need a quality place to live.

🎯 Who would actually rent from us

Travel nurses working at Dosher Memorial and regional hospitals. Remote-work professionals drawn to coastal NC. Trade workers on extended contracts. Young professionals priced out of Wilmington. Teachers, firefighters, and medical staff working in Brunswick County. None of them have upscale, quality workforce housing options at the $1,100/month price point. We do.

🏷️ Something Worth Knowing — The Words Matter

This isn't "subsidized housing." This isn't "low-income housing." This is workforce housing — rental homes for people earning 60-120% of area median income. Teachers. Nurses. Tradespeople. The people who make a community function. Workforce housing triggers better grant eligibility, better tax credits, and better community support than any other framing.

🏛️ Grants & Public Funding

Real public support available to workforce housing that serves the community.

🏆 1. CDBG Infrastructure Play (Strongest — April 16 Hearing)

CDBG can't fund private homes — but it can fund public infrastructure (water lines, sewer extensions, road improvements, stormwater) that benefits both the community AND makes our development feasible. The NC CDBG Neighborhood Revitalization program specifically targets low-to-moderate income areas, which is Navassa.

The April 16 hearing is where the town listens. If community members show up and say "yes, we want sewer extension on Navassa Rd," it enters the town's application. Alicia is attending with the hearing packet.

🏘️ 2. NC Workforce Housing Loan Program (NCHFA)

0% interest, 30-year balloon loan stacked on top of the USDA 538. Specifically designed for workforce rental housing in NC rural areas.

🌾 3. NC Rural Transformation Grant

State grants for rural economic development projects. Housing + commercial event venue (the pavilion) qualifies as economic development.

🔎 4. LIHTC (Low-Income Housing Tax Credit)

9% federal tax credit on qualifying workforce units for 10 years. Reduces the true cost of the project significantly.

🛡️ Protecting the Land for Generations

How the LLC keeps Hooper land in Hooper hands forever.

🛡️ Why the LLC Matters — This Is How We Keep the Land

LBH Properties, LLC already exists with the NC Secretary of State — named for Lillie Bell Hooper, who started all of this, and whose daughter Stephanie Hooper carried it forward. The LLC is in good standing and requires no filings or address updates to move the project forward. Clayton Hooper (Darnell) is the member of record.

North Carolina hasn't adopted the Uniform Partition of Heirs Property Act yet, which means family land can still be forced into sale if one heir wants out. The LLC prevents that. Membership in the LLC transfers through operating agreement — not through default property law — and the land itself never has to be liquidated to satisfy a single heir.

🏡 Family Gathering Rights — Written Into the Agreement

The LBH Properties operating agreement includes provisions for family gathering rights at both lots, access rights during family events, and the 99-year ground lease framework for Tianna + Anthony's home on Lot 1. These rights are written into the legal structure, not left to interpretation.

🌳 The Ground Lease Structure (Lot 1)

Tianna + Anthony own their HOME as real property (after Palm Harbor title surrender). LBH Properties LLC owns the LAND. They pay nominal ground rent ($1/year) for 99 years. USDA 502 accepts this structure. No heir can force partition. The land stays in the LLC forever.

📖 What These Terms Mean — Glossary

Nobody should have to Google while reading the family plan.
🏠 ADU — Accessory Dwelling UnitA legal term for a smaller, separate home on the same property as a main house. NC SB 495 (2025) says every town must allow at least one ADU per lot — they can't say no.
🏗️ IRC — International Residential CodeThe building standard that makes a structure qualify as real estate (not an RV). Mustard Seed's Harvest and Sycamore are IRC-certified. This is what unlocks USDA 538 financing.
🏦 USDA 538 — Guaranteed Rural Rental Housing LoanFederal loan program for multifamily rural rental housing. 90% loan-to-value, 40-year amortization. LLCs can borrow. This is the cornerstone of Lot 2's financing.
🏡 USDA 502 — Guaranteed Rural Home LoanFederal loan program for single-family rural homes. 100% financing, 30-year term, no down payment required. Individual borrowers. Tianna's loan for Lot 1.
🏛️ CDBG — Community Development Block GrantFederal money given to states, which re-grant it to towns for community improvements. The town applies, not individuals. Funds public infrastructure (roads, water, sewer) and housing rehab. At least 70% must benefit low-to-moderate income residents. April 16 hearing is where the town gathers community input.
🏘️ NCHFA / WHLP — NC Housing Finance Agency / Workforce Housing Loan ProgramNC state financing for workforce housing. 0% interest, 30-year balloon. Stacks on top of USDA 538.
📜 SB 495 — Senate Bill 495NC law (2025) requiring every municipality to allow at least one ADU per single-family lot in residential zones. Protects our right to build ADUs as-of-right.
💰 LIHTC — Low-Income Housing Tax CreditFederal program providing a 9% tax credit on qualifying workforce units for 10 years. Reduces project cost substantially.
🛡️ FORTIFIED GoldBuilding standard from the Insurance Institute for Business & Home Safety. 150 MPH wind rating, continuous load path, impact windows, sealed roof deck. 25-50% insurance discount FOR LIFE. Palm Harbor is the only US manufacturer at volume.
🛡️ LLC — Limited Liability CompanyA business structure that protects personal assets. If someone sues the property, they can only go after the LLC's assets — not your personal house, car, or bank account. Also makes it easy to manage family land for generations.
🔐 CollateralSomething of value pledged to secure a loan. Our family LAND serves as collateral for the 538 — replacing cash down payment. The bank holds a lien until the loan is paid off.
👷 Workforce HousingRental housing for people earning 60-120% of area median income — teachers, nurses, tradespeople. Not subsidized. Not low-income. Quality homes at fair rent for the people who keep a community running.
📜 Ground LeaseA long-term recorded lease (typically 99 years) that separates ownership of the home from ownership of the land underneath. Tianna + Anthony own their home; LBH LLC owns the land. USDA 502 accepts this.
🔍 Furnished FinderThe leading platform connecting travel nurses with mid-term (30+ day) furnished rentals. Our Mustard Seed workforce units target this market directly.
🗺️ RMF Zoning — Residential Multi-FamilyThe zoning classification on Parcel 030GB003 that allows multi-unit housing by right. No rezoning, no variance, no conditional use permit needed. This is the single biggest de-risking factor in the entire plan.
